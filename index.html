About Me

Leveraging my passions for learning about tech and solving logical problems, I am studying computer science at Princeton University.

I grew up in Kentucky in a Brazilian and Indonesian household, and I had the privilege to attend the Gatton Academy, a publicly funded STEM boarding school enabling 100 students to live and study at Western Kentucky University for their final two years of high school.

At Gatton, I experienced significant personal growth and cultivated a love for computer science and algorithmic problem solving. I conducted research in the field of theoretical mathematics and presented my work at the national level.

At Princeton, I spend my time playing soccer for the Men's Club Soccer Team, breakdancing with Sympoh Urban Arts, and working at a business nonprofit that connects undergraduates from across the world.
